7 Marketing Experts Reveal The Top Qualities of A Great Content Writer

Discover what industry leaders look for when hiring content writers and how to develop these essential skills.

Christine Miller
Content Strategist & Marketing Consultant
  1. Home
  2. About

In today's digital landscape, content writing has become one of the most sought-after skills in marketing. But what separates a good content writer from a great one? I reached out to seven industry experts to get their insights on the qualities that make content writers truly exceptional.

Whether you're a business owner looking to hire your first content writer or an aspiring writer wanting to improve your skills, these expert insights will help you understand what it takes to succeed in this competitive field.

1. Strategic Thinking Beyond Words

01
"A great content writer doesn't just write—they think strategically about how each piece fits into the bigger marketing picture. They understand the customer journey and can create content that guides prospects through each stage."
— Sarah Johnson, VP of Marketing at TechFlow

The best content writers understand that every piece of content serves a purpose. They know how to align their writing with business objectives, whether that's increasing brand awareness, generating leads, or nurturing existing customers.

2. Research Skills That Go Deep

02
"I can teach someone to write, but I can't teach them to be curious. The best content writers are natural researchers who dive deep into topics and come back with insights that surprise even subject matter experts."
— Marcus Rodriguez, Content Director at Growth Labs

Great content writers are like investigative journalists. They don't just skim the surface—they dig deep, interview experts, analyze data, and uncover unique angles that make their content stand out in a crowded marketplace.

The Research Process

  • Start with competitor analysis to identify content gaps
  • Interview customers and internal stakeholders
  • Use tools like Google Trends and social listening
  • Verify information from multiple credible sources
  • Look for data and statistics to support key points

3. Adaptability Across Formats and Audiences

03
"The content landscape is constantly evolving. A great writer can adapt their style for a LinkedIn post, a technical whitepaper, or a casual blog post—all while maintaining the brand voice."
— Jennifer Lee, Founder of ContentCraft Agency

Versatility is key in modern content marketing. The best writers can switch between different formats, platforms, and audiences without losing their effectiveness. They understand that a tweet requires different skills than a case study.

4. SEO Knowledge That Enhances, Not Hinders

04
"Too many writers either ignore SEO completely or let it dominate their writing. The best content writers know how to weave SEO naturally into compelling content that humans actually want to read."
— David Chen, SEO Consultant at Organic Growth Co.

Understanding SEO is crucial, but great content writers know how to balance search optimization with readability and engagement. They create content that ranks well and converts readers into customers.

5. Emotional Intelligence and Empathy

05
"The best content writers have high emotional intelligence. They can put themselves in their audience's shoes and understand not just what they need to know, but how they're feeling when they need that information."
— Rachel Adams, Psychology-Based Marketing Consultant

Great content writers understand that people don't just buy products—they buy solutions to problems and feelings. They can tap into emotions while providing valuable information, creating content that resonates on a deeper level.

6. Project Management and Reliability

06
"Creative talent is important, but reliability is everything. The writers I work with long-term are the ones who consistently deliver quality work on time and communicate proactively about any challenges."
— Michael Thompson, Marketing Director at SaaS Innovations

Professionalism matters. The best content writers treat their craft like a business, managing their time effectively, meeting deadlines, and communicating clearly with clients and team members.

7. Continuous Learning Mindset

07
"The marketing landscape changes constantly. The content writers who thrive are those who stay curious, keep learning, and aren't afraid to experiment with new approaches and technologies."
— Lisa Wang, Head of Content at Digital Innovation Hub

The most successful content writers are lifelong learners. They stay updated on industry trends, new tools, and changing best practices. They're not afraid to admit when they don't know something and are always looking for ways to improve.

Putting It All Together

While not every content writer needs to excel in all these areas from day one, the most successful professionals develop these qualities over time. If you're hiring a content writer, look for evidence of these traits in their portfolio and during interviews.

If you're a content writer looking to improve, consider which of these areas you could strengthen. The investment in developing these skills will pay dividends throughout your career.

Remember, great content writing is about more than just words on a page—it's about creating value for your audience while achieving business objectives. The writers who master this balance are the ones who build lasting, successful careers in content marketing.
